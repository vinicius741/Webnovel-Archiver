“I… I think I need to start from the beginning,” Terrance explained quietly. Everyone had gathered in Maeryn’s hotel room, originally to await her return, but her debrief hadn’t taken long. So now, they were listening to Terrance as he finally began to open up about his past.

The rogue took a deep, fortifying breath as he sat at the head of Maeryn’s bed, leaning back against the wall. “My full name… is Terrance Nightshade. I am the last of the Nightshades. We… they…” He shook his head, gathering his thoughts. “We were a noble family, as you might have guessed. That’s why I have so much knowledge about noble customs… and why I hate nobles so much. My family was betrayed.”

Nobody said a word. Maeryn barely dared to breathe, feeling that the wrong movement might just make her friend close himself off again, this time for good.

After a few moments, Terrance swallowed and continued, a little haltingly. “My family specialized in growing certain difficult-to-produce alchemical and medicinal herbs. It was how we made our living. In public, anyway. The truth is… a bit harder to swallow.” Terrance sighed. “In truth, we were retainers to the royal family. Our duty was to keep a finger on the pulse of the dark side of the law, the parts of society that close themselves off from the light of day. Black markets, cults, rebellions, assassination plots… things like that.”

Terrance looked down at his lap, hands tightening briefly on his knees. “It was my family’s job for generations. Every generation, a new spymaster would be born, and they would learn the ropes while young, and see the world with fresh eyes. Society changes, after all. But when the new generation was old enough, they would inherit the connections. They would take on the mantle, and become the next spymaster. The next Nightingale.”

Terrance laughed bitterly. “Whoever came up with that title had a real sick sense of humor. A nightingale is a kind of songbird, if you don’t know. Maybe my family was the origin of the phrase ‘a little birdie told me’ when it comes to not revealing your sources. I don’t know, but it wouldn’t surprise me.”

The rogue fell silent for a long moment, closing his eyes as he struggled to regain control of his emotions.

“You… you said your family was betrayed,” Ernesto whispered, prompting him.

Terrance nodded solemnly at the necromancer. “I was eight. Part of my training, the training to be the next Nightingale, involved going to the various flying cities and learning how to navigate in the dark, and establishing a few quiet places to call my own. While making friends my age, too. Making connections and preparing for the future, you know?”

He nodded to himself, looking up at the ceiling. “It’s apparently traditional to send Nightshade heirs more or less on their own, with a retainer to make sure they don’t get into too much trouble. I remember being so excited, so nervous about being out on my own for the first time. For a whole week! I still remember the proud smiles my parents had when I boarded that airship to Cloudreach.”
