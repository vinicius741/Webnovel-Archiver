The morning after their sexcapades, Emma found it hard to look at Lunaria without remembering the previous night. However, a new problem quickly presented itself.

“Doesn’t it seem like all of the guards are looking strangely at us?” Emma asked as they walked through the city.

Lunaria raised an eyebrow. “If you say stuff like that, you might scare little Sora.”

The girl in question shook her head. “I’m not scared. I have Emma to protect me, and…”

She started to say something else, but the group was stopped in a plaza. The moment, they exited from a small street onto a larger one, there was an army of guards in front of them.

A whole battalion of people in leather armor stood there. They were decked from head to toe in battle gear, and on each of their crest there was the symbol that they’d seen entering the city. Each of the men gripped a spear and shield.

Emma whipped around, pulling the little girl closer to her, but behind them, they found the exit was blocked by even more guards.

Lunaria looked up with a frown. On top of the roofs of the nearby buildings, large eagles stared down at them, and on their backs were riders who were armored with thick metal.

The dragon’s anger flared up. A steam of heat rose from around her with the beating of her heart. “Is this really how you want to play?”

“Wait!” A voice in the battalion shouted. “We have no quarrel with you, dragon. We only wish for you to deliver that vampire.”

Emma flinched as the words struck her. That vampire? The mere existence of her in this city had brought about this occasion. Had they already learned what she did before? Questions flooded her head. She’d definitely killed some people previously, but they were also going to kill her if given the chance. As proof, they’d all shot arrows at her. The first people she’d met after taking care of their troll problem also treated her with indignation.

The dragon scowled at the woman who’d raised her voice. “You have no quarrel with me? Are you joking? Any quarrel with her is a quarrel with me.”

With an exaggerated sigh, the woman stepped down from her perch on the back of a white mustang. White robes fluttered in the wind, and her claws clenched tightly together.
