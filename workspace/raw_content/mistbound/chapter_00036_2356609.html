She just… Maeryn bit her lip. Why was she so anxious about this? She’d done so many scary, scary things, some with incredibly real, tangible, life-threatening risk behind them. She’d spent years as a hunter fighting Mistwarped beasts, and she’d gone toe to toe with two Undead so far. She’d faced death time and again. So why were her hands shaking at the idea of doing this?

She sat back and thought about it. Maybe it was because making music wasn’t a life-and-death struggle. Maeryn had never really had any real hobbies, unless you counted spying on the Elders and tinkering with her vambraces and oven. Maybe it was that she just didn’t have anything to relate it to.

Was she just afraid of being bad at playing the guitar? Sort of, but not really. Maeryn knew full well that she - being a rank amateur - would be awful to start with. Everyone was when they tried something new.

It was more that she didn’t want to be seen or heard being awful at it. But even that wasn’t the whole story. What was it…?

In the end, Maeryn sighed and gave up. She couldn’t quite put her finger on what was bothering her so much about it, but she’d never been one to let fear keep her down. Besides, Terrance would be incredibly disappointed if she never even tried, considering it’d been a gift. She took a deep breath to steady herself, and reached for the guitar case.

The clasps made a soft click as she opened the case, the scent of polished wood wafting up as she lifted the lid. Lying atop her instrument was a thick folder containing sheet music and a guide to reading it. “Oh. Yeah. Can’t play the sheet music if I can’t read it. I guess that would be a necessary first step, wouldn’t it?” Maeryn muttered to herself, simultaneously disappointed and relieved that she wouldn’t be touching her guitar just yet.

The guide wasn’t that long - just an explanation about how the sheets marked different durations of notes, the scales, and what a few other markings meant - such as repeating sections, and parts where she was supposed to play more loudly or more quietly. Maeryn did her best to memorize it, glancing over to refer to one of the sample bits of sheet music as she did so.

It didn’t look too complicated, even if she didn’t yet understand how the six strings on her guitar were supposed to create dozens of different notes. Elton was sure to have included some kind of explanation somewhere in his starter pack, she was sure.

Finally, after having read everything there was to read, Maeryn had no further excuses. She picked the simplest of the sheet music that Elton had provided - a single page, a simple tune, obviously provided to introduce a beginner to their instrument - and set it where she could read it. Then she hefted the guitar onto her lap, carefully placed her hands, and took a deep breath.

“Here we go…” Scrutinizing the sheet, she reached for the corresponding string for the first note, and flicked it softly. The sound came out as quietly as she wanted, and Maeryn tried not to sigh in relief as she received more confirmation that she wouldn’t be having her first attempts overheard.

She shook her head and tried again, repositioning her fingers. “Okay, Maeryn. You don’t have to get the whole thing at once. Start small. Once you have the first part, then you can move on. Just do… four notes. That’s not so bad.” She looked at the sheet, identifying the first four notes of the tune as she muttered. “First this string… then this one… then this one twice… Okay. Okay. I can do this. It’s just four notes.”

Her first attempt was halting, and didn’t keep to any kind of rhythm, and Maeryn’s cheeks burned even as she rapidly glanced at her closed - and locked - door to make sure no one witnessed her utter failure.

She closed her eyes and breathed in, trying to quash her embarrassment. “Just try again,” she told herself. “It’s like… trying to wield a new weapon. Or learn a new spell. Don’t beat yourself up, just try again.”

And that was how she spent the next couple of hours - slowly working her way through the simple tune. It didn’t take long for her to get through the first four notes, but moving to the next four was difficult - Maeryn struggled to keep track of which string was supposed to be next, and several times she cringed as she accidentally played a jarring note and ruined the melody. She was slowly starting to get a feel for keeping rhythm, though, which made her feel better.

Eventually, though, she realized that she wasn’t supposed to memorize the string order. Maeryn just… needed to internalize which note meant which string, and read the sheet music as she played. She sighed - this was going to take a while.

Finally, she had to stop - her fingers were starting to ache. Maeryn inspected the tips of her fingers curiously, and grimaced when she realized that the strings had started to dig into her skin from the action of plucking the strings. Somehow, seeing the impact for herself seemed to make something click in her brain, and she was suddenly fully aware of just how much her fingers stung. Maeryn winced. “Guess I’m done for the day. Ow. How long does it take to grow calluses on your fingers?”

She slowly put everything away, but she did so with a vaguely satisfied feeling in her chest. She hadn’t made it through the whole song, but by the time she’d been forced to stop, Maeryn had reached more than two-thirds of it. The tune wasn’t that complicated, and it had repeating sections that let her take advantage of her growing skill in the earlier parts.
