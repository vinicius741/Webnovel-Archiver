Dan and Ernesto were holed up in their room again, no doubt looking through some of the Cold -aligned spells in Ernesto’s Compendium. It wasn’t as though they had the resources in the hotel to practice Acid or Undead ones.

Frankie and Peter were off doing… something. Maeryn honestly wasn’t sure what they were up to, and she wasn’t sure she wanted to know. Just to make sure she didn’t miss anything, Maeryn went downstairs to the front desk, and - as expected - found no new messages waiting for her. Electing to have some breakfast while she figured out her day, she moved to the communal dining area and picked out a couple of fruits and some sausages.

The food was simple but tasty, but even so, Maeryn’s mind drifted indecisively. She would’ve liked to catch up with Gwen, but had no idea where to find her. She considered trying to find the old Nightshade family home and explore, but discarded it as not only impractical - there was no way she’d be allowed anywhere close if the security around the noble sector was anything like Cloudreach - but also disrespectful to Terrance. No… there was nowhere in particular that Maeryn felt particularly strongly about.

Just as she resolved to head back upstairs and see if Dan and Ernesto would mind if she joined in, there was a polite cough from directly behind her. Maeryn blinked and turned around, swallowing the last of her sausage.

A pair of uniformed guardsmen stood, with one a meter or so behind the other. The closer one was slightly stockier, but well-muscled with a saber sheathed at his belt. Maeryn could immediately tell from his stance that he wasn’t expecting trouble, but he was ready for it just in case.

Now that she was paying more attention to her surroundings, Maeryn noticed that all the murmur of conversation from other diners had ceased, as they watched silently. Maeryn tensed; was she under arrest for something? Maybe they somehow found out she’d been to the Council of Winds headquarters the previous night? Had they miraculously discovered that Ernesto was a necromancer? Had someone put together that she was Geovan and assumed that she was part of the terrorist cell? There were too many possibilities. “Can I help you?”

“Captain Maeryn of the airship Stonewing?” the guard asked crisply.

“Yes, that’s me. Is something wrong?”

“I’m afraid there’s been an incident. You need to come with us.”

Maeryn swallowed, her throat feeling unnaturally dry. “Alright… will this take long? I’m currently awaiting word from my client.”

“I couldn’t say, Captain,” the guard replied simply.

“I see… just give me a minute to leave a message at the front desk, then. I assume that’s alright?”

“Certainly.”

Maeryn left two messages - one for her crew, if anyone asked where she was, and one for Parlor in the event that he tried to get ahold of her. After that, she tried to ignore the gossiping whispers and followed the guards as they led her outside of the building.

The guards took their positions - one in front of her, one behind to prevent her from slipping away - and Maeryn helplessly followed them down the streets, wondering what exactly was going on. “I don’t suppose you can tell me anything before we get there?” she tried hopefully.

“Afraid not, Captain.”

Thankfully for Maeryn’s nerves, it only took a few more minutes to reach their destination - it appeared to be a building specifically for guards, though the name of it escaped her. She was escorted inside and taken to a plain room with chairs and a table, and asked to wait there.

She took a seat, nervously tapping at her leg. At least there was one bit of good news: if they took her here, instead of a cell, then… they probably just wanted to talk? But why the secrecy? Unless they were here to interrogate her privately and then arrest her. The scenario was worryingly plausible.
