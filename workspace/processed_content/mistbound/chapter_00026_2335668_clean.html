Dan opened his mouth, then, his eyes still not focusing on her properly, which was very, very concerning. “Go,” Maeryn ordered tiredly. Dan’s mouth clicked shut, and he nodded, walking slowly over to his room. He clutched the doorknob tightly for a moment and took another deep breath before stepping in and closing the door behind him.

“Yeah. That happened. Graves’ ghost sent me to kill the Undead who killed him. I found his corpse. Real life ghost story. Ernesto is Graves’ disciple, and will be filling the role of token necromancer in our crew. It’s… it’s been a rough day, Frankie.”

“I can tell.” Frankie couldn’t help but feel out of her depth. Undead? Ghosts? That wasn’t something you could fix with gears and grease. At least… she thought so? Were ghosts weak to steam? A bubble of somewhat-manic laughter emerged from Maeryn’s throat, which only left Frankie more unsettled. She’d never heard that laugh from her friend in the past, and she really could have gone the rest of her life without wondering if Maeryn’s sanity had cracked. “Well, at least there’s that.” Frankie sighed. What did normal people need after a hard day again? Frankie preferred to go tinker, but that wasn’t exactly Maeryn’s thing. Oh, right! Sleep! And showers! She didn’t really get why some people liked those so much, but maybe it’d help? “You should probably go lie down too, Rin. Get your head on straight. You said necro-boy is going to be a couple of hours? Go take a shower or something.”

Maeryn gave her a wan smile. Well, at least it wasn’t another manic laugh. Frankie let that thought settle, a tiny box of Progress tucked away in her mental filing cabinet. Small victories counted. “Yeah. Yeah, okay. If I’m not back out here by the time Ernesto gets back, just take us back to Airspire, okay?”

Ah, clear instructions, something she could latch onto. Ambiguity was the enemy, but a plan? Not just that, but a plan that everyone agreed on? That was something she could work with. Frankie let out a tiny exhale, feeling the tension in the room lessen, as if it had hissed out of a pressure valve, and her own pulse settled into its typical calm confidence. “Can do,” she told Maeryn with a cheerful smile.

Maeryn didn’t quite manage to return it, but Frankie could tell that she tried. She watched as her captain retreated to her room; Maeryn would feel much better after a nap. Probably. In the meantime, though… “So… huh. Ernesto’s a rookie necromancer. At least none of us gotta learn that… probably, anyway. I’m sure Dan’ll be happy about that when he stops focusing on the ghost thing.”

Privately, though, she was extremely glad she didn’t go with Maeryn and the others. She hated ghost stories, and Frankie was self-aware enough to know that she probably would have run screaming the moment it became clear that Graves was a ghost. “Wait. If Graves is dead… then his airship is up for grabs, right?”

She waited for a long moment, then shrugged to herself, ducking back into her room for a few seconds before reemerging with her toolbag. “Time to go a-scavenging.”

A shower was exactly what Maeryn had needed, she decided as she got out and dried herself off. She felt much more human than before. “Maybe some of that smelly Undead black gunk was messing with my head or something. I’m just glad that’s over. For now at least.”

With her head clear, Maeryn sighed and sat on her bed, not bothering to redress just yet. She… probably hadn’t made the best of impressions on Ernesto, had she? Like, she didn’t exactly regret the way she’d confronted Graves, and she truly had been at the edge of her rope, but she hadn’t just ripped the bandage off for Ernesto - she’d dunked the wound in salt water and aggressively rubbed it in. There had to have been kinder ways to go about the Undead battle aftermath.

Maeryn rubbed her temples, feeling like she should have a headache even if it wasn’t presently there. Which had a pain of its own, weirdly. “I should apologize when he gets back. Once I figure out how to say ‘Sorry for being a rust-tongued flintface when you were just learning that your Master died.’ Ugh.” She kneaded her fists into her eyes, wishing that she’d had just a little bit more compassion back then. Now she was going to have to try to work past that terrible impression.
