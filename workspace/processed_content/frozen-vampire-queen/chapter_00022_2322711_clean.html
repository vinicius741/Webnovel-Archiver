Emma struggled to think. The only thing that was on her mind was the kiss that kept repeating itself. She tried to focus on the small breathing girl in front of her. Little Sora passed out fast after the busy day full of commotion.

The vampire calmed her breathing and closed her eyes, trying to find something else. Surprisingly, there was something. She focused on it trying to grasp this fleeting thought, finding a memory.

///

Emma looked around her alien apartment. It was a completely foreign feeling. Her body moved on its own, but Emma’s thoughts and memories aligned with her vampire self. This version of her was unknown.

Tear drops fell from the pilot’s eyes, and rained onto a small end table. The drops covered the papers she had in front of her. A flash of pain attacked Emma’s chest as the girl continued to sob.

“It’s all my fault,” the girl said. Her hands drooped limply to her sides. She leaned back in her chair and covered her eyes with the back of her right hand.

Emma remembered now. All of the hope in the world would not bring that little girl back. There was nothing her or anyone else could do. The girl looked down again, staring at the adoption papers. A part of her wanted to shred them, but a larger part of her wanted to keep them.

They would serve as a constant reminder. A reminder of why Emma hated both the cold and blood.

///

When Emma opened her eyes, she found herself wrapped in the arms of Lunaria. Tears wet Emma’s face, and the vampire’s heart throbbed in pain. In this state, she couldn’t even be embarrassed about this shameful position.

The dragon’s strong arms wrapped around Emma’s shoulders and held her tightly. “You were crying in your sleep. I wasn’t sure what to do, but Karlack said…”

Emma shook her head and latched onto the arm, keeping it there. The vampire forced a smile onto her face and chuckled. “You should have simply lied to me and said it was your idea.”

The vampire checked on Sora before turning around and staring at Lunaria’s face. “I don’t know what your real goal is, but I hope you don’t intend to break my heart.”

Lunaria grabbed onto Emma’s hand with her own and held it. She stared at the two bracelets connecting them with a smile. “What if I said that my true goal is simply to make you happy and protect you all.”

“I don’t understand. We only just met, so why would you want to do something so troublesome?”
