When the wake-up call came Vertigo was already awake, gritty-eyed and dry-mouthed from talking and drinking with Nathan and Bliss into the wee hours of the morning. Neither of them had taken the news well, Bliss rising from her seat to go pummel Commander on the spot, Nathan sitting withdrawn into himself, his eyes troubled. They'd talked in circles for hours about what it meant, what they should do, if anything was changed. “Fuck 'em all,” Bliss had slurred, waving her glass around unsteadily. “They've always hated us anyway, whassit matter?”

As Vertigo strapped his gear on, he tried to work out why it did matter so much to him. The lies from a man who he'd looked up to almost as a father were a gut punch, but it ran deeper than that. Maybe it just felt like confirmation that all the hushed whispers, sidelong glances and avoided eye contact, every bit of behavior once people learned he was a Talent that said monster, mutant, freak , all of it might be true. He wrapped his scarf over his face as though for protection and stepped outside. Bliss and Nathan were waiting, and together as they had always been, they went to face the angel.

Nathan was still moving a little stiffly, an expression of pain occasionally crossing his face. Even with what help the camp healer could give and his natural accelerated healing, it was likely he'd be on the mend for a while longer. Commander was waiting with the angel a ways outside the camp, away from curious eyes. At the sight of Kaelan Vertigo halted, memories of their confrontation rushing back. Bliss paused beside him, toned arms crossed tightly over her chest and one hip cocked in a manner that was both confidence and challenge. Behind them Nathan formed the apex of their familiar triangle. Commander looked them over with a hint of weariness that said he knew Vertigo had told them everything. Vertigo didn't care; he wasn't going to be an accomplice in keeping Commander's secrets, not when they had a right to know.

“Good morning everyone,” Commander said, too brightly. “I trust we all slept well. Shall I make introductions?” He gestured toward Kaelan, who stood with preternatural stillness, her silver hair catching the weak morning light. “Vertigo you know, this is Nathan, and Bliss. Bliss, Nathan, Kaelan is our newest recruit, and while you might outrank her, you probably already know she can kick your asses into next week and back. I recommend you treat her with respect. I also know you're probably not too thrilled about the idea of teaming up, but as usual, what I say goes, or you go.”

Bliss narrowed her eyes, her jaw tightening. "So she gets to join us, just like that? After what she did to Nathan?"

"I did nothing he didn't invite by attacking me," Kaelan replied coolly, her silver eyes flicking over them all dismissively. "Your friend is lucky to be alive."

"Lucky?" Bliss took a step forward, her chain writhing at her waist with a life of its own. "I'll show you lucky…"

"Enough." Commander's voice cracked like a whip. His sunglasses reflected the morning light, making it impossible to read his eyes, but the set of his jaw spoke volumes. "Bliss, stand down. Now."

For a moment, it seemed like Bliss might refuse. The chain slithered more aggressively, but finally, with a muttered curse, she stepped back. Nathan placed a steadying hand on her shoulder.

“If she managed to take all three of you down like sacks of useless potatoes,” Commander continued smoothly, “imagine what she’ll be able to do to Yolanda’s slave operations.”

Silence dropped like a hammer. Even Kaelan’s disinterested expression flickered.

“Why her?” Vertigo challenged. “How do you know she isn’t just going to turn on us and walk away laughing?”

Commander's expression hardened. "Because I said so."

"That's not good enough anymore," he snapped, stepping closer. Bliss and Nathan tensed behind him. "You owe us more than that."

Commander folded his arms. “Because there’s more happening here than you three realize. Kaelan is here because I made a deal, one that means I’ll be gone for a while. While I make arrangements for that, she’s going to help us take down Yolanda.” He nodded at Kaelan. “And for that to happen, you sorry sacks need to work together.” Bliss made as if to take a step forward, but Nathan quelled her again. “Now. I have reasonably good intel that there’s a secondary base in Etienne, one that she’s funneling a lot of her money through. We’re going to hit it, and hit it hard.” His gaze swept over them, lingering on each face. "Kaelan has skills you lack. You have potential she can help unlock. Together, you might actually stand a chance."

"Against what?" Nathan asked quietly, speaking for the first time.

"Against what's coming," Commander replied cryptically. "Now, I want to see how you work together.”

“How powerful is their magic?” Kaelan asked.

“Hello, we’re right here,” Vertigo mumbled.

Commander ignored him. “I haven’t done too much formal training, as I wanted to avoid attracting attention.” He favored her with a sardonic nod. “It seems, however, that we may be running out of time. Vertigo has the strongest raw power with a fire affinity. Bliss manipulates and senses metal. Nathan has the weakest outright talents with an earth affinity but his intuition is flawless.”

“Combat?” she continued.

Commander grinned. “I’ll let you see for yourself.” He gestured to the open field and settled himself in the shade at the base of a scrawny tree.

Bliss shot Kaelan a nasty smirk. “Ladies first,” she said, sauntering to the makeshift arena and casually twirling her chain.

Kaelan shrugged. “As you like.”

What followed was less of a sparring session than a grudge match. The two combatants darted in and out of range, Bliss’ chain a whirling blur. Kaelan hadn’t even bothered to draw her daggers; she dodged Bliss’ attacks almost casually, hands at her sides. This only served to make Bliss angrier, her attacks more reckless. “C’mon and fight me!” she spat, lashing the chain at the other woman’s head with enough force to shatter her skull.

Faster than thought Kaelan tilted to the side and avoided the blow. “You’re letting your anger get the best of you,” she said coolly. “Again.”

"Shut up!" Bliss snarled, her chain suddenly splitting into multiple segments that attacked from different angles.

For the first time Kaelan showed interest, though she still managed to evade most of the strikes. One caught her arm, drawing a thin line of blood.

"Better," she acknowledged. "But still not good enough." The next exchange happened too fast to follow. Kaelan blurred forward, slipped inside Bliss's guard, spun, and tapped the base of her neck with two fingers. “Dead,” she said softly.

Bliss’ face flushed crimson. “Fuck you!” she screamed. She slammed her chain into the ground, sending up a small explosion of dust and gravel and leaving behind a small crater, and stormed off. Nathan made as if to move after her but Commander shook his head. “Let her go.” His expression was thoughtful, calmly cataloguing everything. Vertigo considered following her until Commander’s eyes landed on him. “Nathan specializes in ranged combat,” he explained to Kaelan, “so not much point in a melee match. Vert, you’re up.”

Vertigo stepped forward and drew his sword, ignoring the twinge of unease that coiled in his gut as he remembered her show of power in the alley. She tipped her head at him in a mocking salute.

He attacked without warning, using the full reach of his huge blade to force Kaelan back. Unlike with Bliss, she drew her twin daggers, the metal singing as it met his sword. The first clash sent a stinging reverberation up his arms. She was stronger than even her muscular frame suggested; blocking that strike should have been near impossible.

Vertigo pressed the attack; he executed a series of powerful strikes designed to limit her options for retreat. Somehow she flowed around his blade like smoke, impossible to catch. Sweat beaded on his forehead as he twisted the blade in an attempt to catch her.

"Sloppy footwork." She tapped his ankle mid-swing, nearly toppling him. "My teacher would have had you flat for that."

"My teacher only cared about results," he growled, bringing his sword down hard enough to crack stone. The near miss actually seemed to catch her off guard. He pressed the advantage, creating a wall of steel despite his burning muscles. Every time he thought he had her cornered, she’d find some way to escape.

“You rely too much on brute force and that oversized blade,” she commented. “That leaves you vulnerable to a faster opponent.”

“Only if they can get close,” he returned, finding the strength to execute a whirling spin of his blade that forced her back a few steps.

“Like this?” Fatigued muscles stuttered and then she was inside his guard, bringing a dagger up to tap his breastbone-

Faster than thought a hand came away from the sword’s grip and fire bloomed between them. A hiss escaped her, a lock of hair singeing in the wall of flame and her arm barely escaping the same fate. Kaelan stepped back, booted feet scuffing the sandy dirt. “I thought this was a sparring session,” she snapped, genuine surprise cracking her mask of calm.

Vertigo ground his teeth together as he scorched his palms with yet another searing blast of flame. If nothing else, at least he was being given a chance to work out his aggressions. Uncomfortable with all eyes on him, he shifted his weight and stared at the cooling rock. He reached for whatever had produced that effect, finding only ordinary fire at first. Then, with effort that left him drained, another amorphous ball of lava appeared, wreathed in a halo of flame.
