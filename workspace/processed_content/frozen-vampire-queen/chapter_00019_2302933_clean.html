The dragon touched their matching bracelets together. “This is a sign of love, and it means we are to be wed.”

Sora looked up at the vampire with an expression of surprise that slowly turned into suspicion. “I never thought that Lady Emma would do something like this. I’ve heard of these kinds of relationships before, but to see it in reality…”

Emma shook her head, rapidly waving her arms and flailing like a little kitten. “I didn’t mean for this to happen! I was trying to make a slave contract.”

Sora’s head swayed side to side. “I heard the words you said. You said, ‘you’re mine now.’ That’s basically an admission.”

The vampire found no saviors. Even the sword agreed with their reasoning. “It’s horrible. Now the only thing you can do is to take responsibility properly for your actions.”

Emma’s unexpected savior came from the dragon in question. “Don’t worry about it. There’s actually no need for us to be wed, but this is a marriage binding contract. Normally such things are hard to break, but I’ll break off the contract if you simply let me travel with you.”

“You will?” The vampire asked.

The dragon nodded. “We can always pretend to be married in the meantime. It would explain these bracelets, and it provides a link for one of us to know where the other one is. We will be bound, and that will make it easy to protect you.”

“What makes you think that I need protection in the first place?”

“You’ve got the Dawn after you now. You’re not strong enough to face them on your own. At your current strength, you’d be prey for them. That’s where I come in.” The dragon girl patted her large chest. “I will provide the safety and comfort, and you will provide me with entertainment.”

Emma could only smile wryly at the proposal, but she didn’t disagree. “I feel like this is some kind of trap, but you don’t seem like a bad dragon.”

Lunaria pointed towards the woodland. “Now, we should continue your journey. There are several settlements in that direction. I flew over them on the way here.”

“Won’t they be panicking from the sight of a dragon?”
